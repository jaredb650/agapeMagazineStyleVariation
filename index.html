Editor’s Note / Opening Spread

We do not curate lineups. We build rituals.

Agape lives in the hour when the room stops behaving like a venue and starts behaving like a body. The floor is the text. The bass is the margin. The air is printed with whatever the crowd can carry.

We come from basements, warehouse corners, improvised booths, and all the places where a night has to be earned before it can be remembered. The point is not polish. The point is charge.

Every event is a page torn from a larger issue. Every resident is part of the recurring cast. Every person in the room becomes part of the archive.

— The Agape Collective / New York City
